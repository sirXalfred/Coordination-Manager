## 1. Introduction

This trademark policy ("Policy") explains how you may use the trademarks, service marks, and logos of Voltaire Swarm OÜ ("we", "us", "our") in connection with the Coordination Manager software project ("the Project").

The Project's source code is available under the MIT License. That copyright license does **not** include any right or license to use our trademarks. This Policy explains how to use our marks consistent with trademark law and community expectation.

---

## 2. Trademarks Covered by This Policy

This Policy covers:

1. **Word marks** ("Word Marks"):
   - "Coordination Manager"
   - "Voltaire Swarm"

2. **Logos** ("Logos"):
   - Any official Coordination Manager logo or graphical mark distributed by Voltaire Swarm OÜ.

Collectively, these are the "Marks". This Policy covers our Marks whether or not they are formally registered as trademarks in any jurisdiction.

## 3. General Use Guidelines

Whenever you use one of the Marks, you must do so in a way that does not mislead anyone -- directly or by omission -- about what they are getting and from whom.

For example:
- You may not claim to distribute "Coordination Manager" software when you are distributing a modified version of it.
- You may not use our Logo on a website in a way that suggests the site is official or that we endorse it.

You may, of course:
- Say you like Coordination Manager.
- Say you participate in or contribute to the Coordination Manager project.
- State truthfully that you are running or providing an unmodified version of Coordination Manager.

You may not use the Marks in connection with software that is not in compliance with the MIT license for that software.

You may not use or register the Marks, in whole or in part, as part of your own trademark, service mark, domain name, company name, trade name, product name, or service name.

Trademark law does not allow use of names too similar to ours. The following kinds of uses would be considered too similar:
- `CoordinationManagerPro`
- `MyCoordinationManager`
- `VoltaireSwarmCloud`
- Phonetic or translated equivalents intended to evoke our Marks.

## 4. Use for Software

### 4.1 Uses we consider non-infringing

#### 4.1.1 Redistribution of unmodified source code or unmodified executables

When you redistribute an unmodified copy of Coordination Manager in accordance with the MIT license, you are not changing its quality or nature. You may -- and should -- retain the Word Marks and any official Logos on that redistribution.

This applies only when the redistribution is an exact copy of an official Project release.

#### 4.1.2 Distribution of modified software

You may use the Word Marks (but not the Logos) to truthfully describe the origin of modified software you distribute. For example:

> "This software is derived from the source code of Coordination Manager."

You should place your own name, mark, or logo on any version to which you have made substantive modifications, since by modifying the software you are the origin of that exact version.

#### 4.1.3 Statements about compatibility, interoperability, or derivation

You may use the Word Marks (but not the Logos) to truthfully describe the relationship between your software and ours. Use the Mark after a verb or preposition that describes the relationship:

- Acceptable: "Alice's plugin for Coordination Manager"
- Not acceptable: "Alice's Coordination Manager plugin" (used as part of a product name)

We do not currently operate a formal "Coordination Manager compatible" certification program.

#### 4.1.4 Distribution of self-built executables (limited use of Logos)

You may use the Word Marks and Logos for distribution of source or executable code on the condition that:

- The executable is built from the official Project source code; and
- Any modifications are limited to:
  - Toggling features already included in the software,
  - Translations into other languages, or
  - Incorporating upstream bug-fix patches.

Any other modification requires you to drop the Logos and clearly identify your fork by a different name.

### 4.2 Uses requiring permission

The following uses require written permission from Voltaire Swarm OÜ:

- Using the Marks as part of a domain name or subdomain (for example, `coordinationmanager.example.com`).
- Using the Marks in connection with paid services, hosting, or consulting offered to third parties.
- Using the Marks in connection with a conference or commercial event.

To request permission, see [Section 6](#6-contact).

## 5. Use for Non-Software Goods and Services

### 5.1 Publications and presentations

You may use the Word Marks in titles of books, articles, blog posts, talks, and similar publications, and the Logos as illustrations within those documents, provided the use does not suggest that we have authored, endorsed, or agreed with your work.

### 5.2 User groups and community events

You may use the Word Marks in the name of a community user group provided that:

- The main focus of the group is Coordination Manager;
- The group is not operated for profit; and
- Any charges for attendance are limited to covering the actual cost of venue, food, and drink.

You may not use or register the Marks as part of your own organisation name, domain name, company name, or product name without prior written approval.

## 7. General Information

### 7.1 Trademark marking and legends

The first or most prominent mention of a Mark in your work should be accompanied by the unregistered trademark symbol ("(TM)") until such time as we register the mark and notify the community.

Where the use of a Mark is permitted under this Policy, please include the following notice on the same page or in equivalent documentation:

> "Coordination Manager(TM) and Voltaire Swarm(TM) are trademarks of Voltaire Swarm OÜ. Used with permission."

### 7.2 Proper trademark use

The following conventions apply to all trademarks, not just ours:

- Distinguish trademarks from surrounding text using initial capital letters: "Coordination Manager", not "coordination manager".
- Use the trademark in its exact form, not abbreviated, hyphenated, or combined with other words.
- Do not pluralise a trademark.
- Use a trademark as an adjective modifying a noun: "the Coordination Manager application", not "a Coordination Manager".
- Do not translate the trademark into other languages.

### 7.3 Use of Logos

When use of a Logo is permitted under this Policy:

- You may scale the Logo but may not change its colors, proportions, or shape, add elements, distort it, or combine it with other logos.
- If the context requires black-and-white reproduction and the Logo is in colour, you may render the Logo in monochrome.
